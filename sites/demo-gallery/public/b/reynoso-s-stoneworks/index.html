The craft

A family trade, one stone at a time.

Reynoso’s Stoneworks is a family-owned masonry shop that has worked Sonoma County, California’s homes and gardens since 2000. The work is the old kind: natural stone, brick and block fitted by hand, with reinforced concrete where it counts — built to traditional standards, not rushed.

Jose Reynoso treats every wall like it carries the family name — because sometimes it literally does. That care is why so much of their work is neighbor-to-neighbor across Santa Rosa and the wider county.

“ We’d rather lay one wall that lasts a generation than ten that don’t. — the Reynoso family

What we build

Stone, brick and block — whatever the job calls for.

One family crew across the full range of masonry, so the same hands that pour the footing set the last cap stone.

01

Natural & Manufactured Stone Veneer

Stone facing for entryways, fireplaces, accent walls and exterior facades — fieldstone, ledgestone and cut stone, fit tight and true.

02

Retaining Walls & Hardscapes

Engineered retaining and garden walls built for the slope they hold — drained, reinforced and laid to stand for the long haul.

03

Paver Patios, Walkways & Flagstone

Paver patios, flagstone paths and walkways set on a proper base — outdoor living areas built level, drained and made to last.

04

Brick & Block Masonry

Structural and decorative brickwork, CMU block walls and garden walls — load-bearing or finish work, mortared by hand.

05

Fireplaces, Columns & Water Features

Stone fireplaces, chimneys, pillars, fountains and entry columns — the focal-point pieces that give a property its character.

06

Foundations, Concrete & Repairs

Reinforced concrete and foundation work, plus repairs and repointing — restoring old masonry as carefully as building new.

How it goes

Four steps, no surprises.

  1. 1

    Free estimate & advice

    Call Jose Reynoso and walk the site together. You get a straight estimate — and honest masonry advice — at no charge.

  2. 2

    Materials & layout

    We help you choose the right stone, brick or paver for the look and the load, then set the lines so it sits right before a single course goes down.

  3. 3

    Built by hand

    Proper base and footing, reinforced where it matters, then stone fit and mortared by hand — the part you can't rush and we don't.

  4. 4

    Built to outlast us

    We leave a clean site and work that's made to stand for decades — the kind of wall a neighbor points to and asks who built it.
