The name

Thirty-two points on the compass.

Named for the thirty-two points of the mariner's compass — the winds early navigators followed across the globe in search of new places, people and flavor.

The compass rose carries the spirit of the first explorers — and the conviction behind every bottle: to sail past the easy choice and source only the best fruit Sonoma County and its neighbors will give. Each wind a vineyard. Each bearing a vintage.

The Mascarin legacy

From Friuli to a creek outside Healdsburg.

Four generations of one Italian family, each handing the next a deeper love of the vine — from the boats out of northern Italy to the rows of Dry Creek Valley.

  1. 1904 First generation

    Angelo Mascarin

    Angelo emigrates from northern Italy and is hired to plant and farm vineyards in central California for the Italian Swiss Colony — now Asti Winery. The family's roots in California wine begin in the soil.

  2. 1930s–40s Second generation

    Cellar master at Roma Wine Co.

    Ed's father — an Italian immigrant who married Angelo's daughter — serves as cellar master at the Roma Wine Company in Kingsburg, working alongside Louis Martini and the Cella brothers during the formative decades of California wine.

  3. 2009 Third generation

    Ed Mascarin

    After a Cal Berkeley education, a turn as a college baseball star and a 40-year career in industrial engineering, Ed comes full circle to the family trade and becomes proprietor of 32 Winds — building it into Mascarin Family Wines.

  4. 2010 Fourth generation

    Mia Mascarin-Oven

    Ed's daughter joins the family business after 20+ years in the energy industry, leading operations, strategy and sales — carrying the Mascarin name into its fourth generation.

Visit the creek

Olive trees, gardens, and the Dry Creek deck.

Two miles from the Healdsburg Plaza, the tasting room sits creekside among towering olive trees. Every visit is seated, unhurried and by appointment.

60 min · 1–6 guests · $45 / person

A guided flight of five hand-picked wines from sustainably farmed Sonoma County vineyards, served table-side among towering olive trees and gardens, or on the deck overlooking Dry Creek.
