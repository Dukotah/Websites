The argument

The building envelope is one system. Most jobs split it across two trades.

A wall is a stack of layers, and a normal project hands part of it to an insulator and part of it to a builder — two licenses, two schedules, a seam where blame lives. We hold both classifications, so the whole section is ours.

  1. B

    Exterior finish & sheathing

    The weather skin — siding, sheathing, the parts that shed rain.

  2. B

    Framing — studs, plates, headers

    The skeleton. Walls, openings, load paths, additions.

  3. C-2

    Insulation — batt · blown-in · spray foam

    The thermal break. R-value that meets Title-24.

  4. B

    Slab & subfloor

    What everything stands on, tied to the footing.

  5. B

    Concrete foundation & footing

    Below grade. Where the load finally rests.
