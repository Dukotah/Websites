The hand behind it

Fifteen years on the wall before the first sign went up.

Angel Brothers started with Ivan Angel — a journeyman lather and plasterer who put in fifteen years on the scaffold before founding the company in 2014. That trade-first habit still runs the job: the crew keys its own lath, floats its own brown coat, and works a finish wet edge-to-edge so a wall reads true in raking light.

How a finish goes on

Four coats. No shortcuts between them.

  1. 01

    Lath & scratch

    Self-furring lath over a weather-resistive membrane, then a scratch coat keyed by hand so every later coat has something to grip.

  2. 02

    Brown coat

    A true, flat brown coat floated to plane — the difference between a wall that reads straight in raking light and one that doesn't.

  3. 03

    Finish coat

    The texture you actually see — sand, Santa Barbara, smooth or lace — worked wet, edge-to-edge, so there's no cold joint mid-wall.

  4. 04

    Cure & seal

    Controlled cure, then color or sealer where the system calls for it, so the finish keeps its tone through Sonoma summers.
