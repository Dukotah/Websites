The land

The same hills. Four generations of saying yes.

Long before it held a single ceremony, Glenn Ranch was simply home — worked, loved and handed down through 4 generations of one Petaluma family. The barns weathered. The oaks grew tall. The light learned its way across the fields.

Today that same ground is kept for weddings — a working family ranch five minutes outside the city of petaluma, opened up so another family can begin here too. Nothing about it is staged. The history is real, and on your day it quietly becomes part of yours.

The spaces

One ranch, every part of the day.

From the morning calm of the getting-ready barns to the last song in the reception barn, the whole day lives on a single piece of land — no shuttles, no second locations, no rush.

01

The ceremony grounds

Choose your spot. Several ceremony sites sit scattered across the property — under the oaks, out in the open field, with the hills falling away behind you. Two arches are yours to use.

02

The reception barn

The heart of the night. A true old barn strung with warm lights — long tables, two bars, wine barrels and a dance floor that has held a lot of first dances.

03

Two getting-ready suites

Separate barns for each side of the party to gather, get ready and take their time before the first look.

04

The grounds & golden hour

Rolling hills, a long oak-lined drive, dry-gold grass and the kind of late light photographers chase. Mark will even drive you up for sunset photos.
