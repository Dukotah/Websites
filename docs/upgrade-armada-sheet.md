- **Impact**: as perceived by a cold prospect opening the demo link
- **Key-free / needs-key**: KEY-FREE runs with no env vars · needs-key items flagged with cost

- Tasks within a day are ordered: do top-to-bottom

## If You Only Do 3 Things

These three deliver the largest perceived-quality jump per hour invested and directly address the three biggest "AI slop" tells a cold prospect recognizes in under five seconds:

**1. Day 1 tasks 1.1–1.2: CTA micro-interactions + section scroll-reveals (~60 lines of CSS/TS)**
Static pages that snap instead of easing are the most reliable "AI template" perceptual trigger in 2026. Eight lines of CSS on `.btn` (spring-curve transition, Y lift on hover, faster active) plus a 12-line IntersectionObserver extending the existing `reveal.ts` make every scroll feel custom-built. Zero dependencies, zero API keys, works in every browser. This is the single highest ROI change in the whole sheet.

**2. Day 3 tasks 3.1–3.3: Fix gallery bug + wire Openverse + smartcrop**
Two existing bugs silently cap `realPhotos` at 10–15 pts on ~30% of businesses: Wikimedia/credited photos are rejected from `galleryImages` even when zero own photos exist, and Openverse is built but never called. Fixing both is code-only (no new packages beyond `smartcrop-sharp`), takes a few hours, and immediately unlocks +7 pts on thin-data businesses — the exact sites where the demo needs the most help.

**3. Day 4 task 4.2 + Day 8 task 8.1: Scrape Google star ratings + produce the before/after Lighthouse proof**
A `★ 4.8 · 312 Reviews` badge in the hero is the single highest-converting trust element for local service businesses. Scraped key-free via Playwright from Google's public SERP snippet. Paired with the before/after Lighthouse artifact (`scripts/before-after-proof.mjs`), the cold email line becomes: `"Your current site scores 41 perf / 62 a11y. Here's what it looks like fixed: [demo link]. Their demo scores 94 / 98."` This transforms the pitch from aesthetic to data-backed — the difference between a prospect clicking out of curiosity and one picking up the phone.
